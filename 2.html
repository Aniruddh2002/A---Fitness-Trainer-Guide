Legs Exercise

Leg exercises help build muscles. Bigger muscles require more energy to move around, so you burn more calories in the process. In fact, if using calories is the goal, strength training is the way to go. It performs better than all the following when it comes to keeping your metabolism.

Squats

Squats are ideal if you have back problems. Since they’re done while standing up and without extra weight, they won’t strain the back. For balance or extra support, perform your squats standing alongside a wall or next to a chair or the edge of a table with one hand on the object. Resist the urge to pull on it or push off from it.

Plank leg lifts

Regular planks target the upper body, core, and hips. You can add leg lifts to strengthen your butt and upper legs..

Step-ups

Step-ups are like one-legged squats. The repetitive movement will work your thighs, hips, and butt. You’ll need a knee-height plyometric box or raised platform. To limit stress on the knee, always step onto the center of the box.

Stability ball knee tucks

Doing knee tucks on a stability ball will tone legs fast. It works your calves, shins, and abs. For this exercise, you’ll need a stability ball that’s properly inflated.

Box jumps

You can also do box jumps on a plyometric box. This explosive workout is one of the best ways to tone your legs, butt, and core. When you land on the box, drop your hips to absorb the force. Don’t lock your knees and quads. This can hurt your knees.

Speedskater jumps

Speedskater jumps, or lateral jumps, engage your leg muscles. This high-intensity move also improves your lunging power and ability. Start with small jumps. Over time, you can try bigger jumps.

Bridge

The bridge tones your hips, thighs, butt, and core. To make it harder, wrap a resistance band around your thighs.
